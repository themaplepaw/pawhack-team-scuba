Pet healthcare 🩺

Where to go when your buddy needs a check-up — or help right now. All clinics here are Singapore-based.

🚨 Is it an emergency?

Head to a 24-hour clinic straight away (call ahead so they can prepare) if you see any of these:

  • Trouble breathing
  • Seizures
  • Uncontrolled bleeding
  • Suspected poisoning
  • Can't walk or pee
  • Major injury / fall
  • Non-stop vomiting
  • Bloated, hard belly

Finding a regular vet & staying licensed
📋

Singapore Veterinary Association

The professional body for vets in Singapore — a trustworthy starting point for finding registered practitioners.

› 🐶

AVS pet care & health guides

Official guidance on vaccinations, sterilisation, microchipping and keeping pets healthy in Singapore.

› 🪪

Licence your pet (PALS)

All dogs & cats must be licensed. Cat licensing is free until 31 Aug 2026 — do it before the deadline.

›
⚠️ Clinic details, hotlines and opening hours can change. Always call ahead before heading down, and confirm the latest info on the clinic's own website. In a poisoning emergency, bring the packaging or a photo of what your pet ate.

🕊️ Memorial & aftercare

🌈 A gentle goodbye

In Singapore, pet burial is only allowed on private property, so cremation is the usual choice. These providers handle farewells with care, and many offer keepsakes and grief support. Take your time — there's no rush.
